## Key Points

- Some problems in work are not intellectually complex but are difficult because of their sheer size and scope.
- Examples include auditing 3,000 vendor contracts for compliance updates, migrating a 2-million-line COBOL codebase, and reviewing an entire quarter of customer interactions for churn signals.
- In these cases, the reasoning at each individual step is simple and within the capability of any competent person.
- The real difficulty lies in maintaining sustained attention, consistency, and thoroughness across a very large surface area of work.
- The main risk in such problems is dropping details or losing quality as scale increases.
- The speaker states that these large, effort-heavy problems are exactly what Agentyc was designed to address.

## Notes

### Nature of “effort problems”
- The speaker describes a class of work problems that are not intellectually challenging in the sense of requiring complex reasoning or deep expertise.
- Instead, they are challenging because they are **large in scope** and require extensive, repetitive effort.
- Each individual action within these problems is straightforward, but the aggregate workload is enormous.

### Concrete examples of large-scope tasks
- **Auditing contracts:** One example is auditing 3,000 vendor contracts to accommodate compliance changes. Each contract audit step is conceptually simple, but the scale (thousands of contracts) turns it into a demanding effort problem.
- **Legacy code migration:** Another example is migrating a legacy codebase consisting of 2 million lines of COBOL. Understanding or changing a single part may be easy, but doing it reliably across millions of lines is labor-intensive.
- **Customer interaction review:** A third example is reviewing every customer interaction from the last quarter to identify churn signals. Evaluating a single interaction is simple; processing an entire quarter’s worth becomes burdensome.

### What actually makes these problems hard
- The speaker emphasizes that **“the thinking at each step is super straightforward”** and within the competence of any capable person.
- The real challenge is maintaining **sustained attention and thoroughness** across a huge surface area of work.
- As scope explodes, the main risk becomes **dropping details**—missing items, overlooking inconsistencies, or gradually lowering the standard of review.

### Agentyc’s intended fit
- The speaker concludes by stating that these types of large, effort-intensive, detail-sensitive problems are **“the problems Agentyc always built for.”**
- This positions Agentyc as a solution tailored to tasks where the bottleneck is human stamina and consistency, rather than raw intellectual difficulty.
